With a background that blends biotechnology, zoology, and bioinformatics, I enjoy translating biological questions into computational solutions. March 2025 Journal of University of Shanghai for Science and Technology

A Review on Immunoinformatics-Driven Multi-Epitope Vaccine Approaches for Dengue Virus (DENV)

This review discusses the role of immunoinformatics and multi-epitope vaccine design in overcoming the challenges associated with dengue virus vaccine development. Dengue virus (DENV), with its four serotypes, poses a significant challenge for conventional vaccine approaches due to the risk of antibody-dependent enhancement (ADE). The paper explores how computational tools for epitope prediction, antigenicity scoring, population coverage analysis, and molecular docking can be integrated into a rational vaccine design pipeline to develop safe and broadly protective multi-epitope vaccine candidates against DENV.

February 2025 Journal of University of Shanghai for Science and Technology

Biology and Damage Dynamics of the Banana Skipper, Erionota torus (Evans) (Lepidoptera: Hesperiidae)

This research investigates the life cycle, morphology, infestation patterns, and agricultural impact of Erionota torus (Evans) on banana cultivation. The banana skipper butterfly is a significant pest of banana plantations across South and Southeast Asia. The study documents the biology of the pest — from egg to adult — and characterises the nature and extent of foliar damage caused during larval feeding stages, providing insight into infestation dynamics and their implications for crop management.
